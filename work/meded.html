Simulation for Medical Training

The medical community can seem intimidating for those who aren't apart of it. However, this should not be the case. They, like most of us, are just people trying to solve problems. For this project, I was the sole UXer on a large team of mostly software engineers and sales reps attempting to revolutionize the way med students learn and examine life-saving skills using human simulators.

TASK FLOW

01 / Memorable Personas

Most of our users were medical students – meaning learners of a very specific set of skills within an environment having a peculiar educational structure– just like Hogwarts.

02 / Better Understanding

Having all of the personas as part of a theme in which most (if only somewhat familiar with JK Rowling) could understand the relationships and hierarchy made for a set of instantly understandable user roles.

03 / In Practice

This simplified what was a mystification of confusing permissions and task discussions down to a clear organizational structure, transferable to workflow creation on the technical side.

INTROSPECTION

"BY DESIGN"

Condensing my findings into a keynote and presenting it to the company in an informal and participatory fashion fostered an environment where everyone could place themselves in the user's shoes. This created an organic session of introspection and underlined the importance of understanding our audience first, rather that just making a prettier, faster version of the current application.
